<p style="margin-top: 0px;">There&nbsp;is a range of information&nbsp;that Australian Government agencies are required to publish online.</p>
<h2 style="margin-bottom: 0.2em; font-weight: normal; letter-spacing: -0.04em; color: #377690; font-size: 1.95em;">Why must I?</h2>
<p style="margin-top: 0px;">Government agencies&nbsp;must publish a range of information on their websites, including information that is required to be published&nbsp;as part of &nbsp;the Information Publication Scheme (IPS) under the<em>Freedom of Information Act 1982.&nbsp;</em>The IPS came into effect on 1 May 2011<em>.</em></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0px;">You must consider the IPS and the Online Content Requirements when publishing information on your website.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0px;">The Online Content Requirements have been updated to reflect the changes under the IPS.&nbsp;They supersede the Online Information Service Obligations (OISOs) released in 2001. The OISOs were originally published as part of the 2000 Government Online Strategy.</p>
<h2 style="margin-bottom: 0.2em; font-weight: normal; letter-spacing: -0.04em; color: #377690; font-size: 1.95em;">What must I do?</h2>
<p style="margin-top: 0px;">The following information must be available on Government agency websites:</p>
